About Resistor

A resistor is a simple electrical part with two ends that controls how much electricity flows in a circuit. Its job is to decrease the electricity flow and lessen the voltage in a specific part of the circuit. It's usually made of coiled copper wires around a ceramic rod, with an insulating paint covering the outside.

In a resistor:

Voltage (V): The force that pushes electric charge through it.

Current (I): The flow of electric charge through the resistor.

Resistance (R): The resistor's opposition to current flow.

They're related by Ohm's Law: V = I Ã— R. More voltage means more current, while higher resistance reduces current for a given voltage.
